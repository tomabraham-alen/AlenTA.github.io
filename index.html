Master's thesis, ISAE-SUPAERO
June 2022 - present

The research project aims to deal with high dimensional problems within the scope of reliability analysis. The major actor in the flow of project is Python, specifically the OpenTurns package. We aim to incorporate adaptive Kriging with Importance Sampling to create an efficient Kriging model. It is approached step by step by initially working on a benchmark problem of cantilever beam deflection. The benchmark is the default deflection problem given on OpenTurns documentation. We were able to check and reporduce reliable results by the Adaptive Kriging model based on Monte Carlo Simulation (AK-MCS) in comparison with the results from OpenTurns which is based on Importance Sampling, thus confirming the validity of the AK-MCS model. Next step is to enhance the model by creating an AK-IS model followed by the most challenging part of the project that is to accommodate high dimensional scenarios in the model which is still considered an open problem thus giving us the scope of novelty in the work. The below work is a result of combined synergic work with my colleague Gokulnath R, and our mentors Christian Gogu (Prof, ISAE-SUPAERO) & Gabriele Capasso (Airbus).

Summer Internship, ISAE-SUPAERO
June 2023 (2 months)

With growing interest in exploring sustainability options for the aviation of the future, I applied to work on a project which studies the feasibility of a solar powered light aircraft falling under CS-23. The work was carried out by parallely working on constraint analysis for the defined TLARs and doing extensive literature survey on solar PV technology. Topics varying from basic types of panels to schockley queisser limit to future scope of peak panel efficiency were studied. The solar panel tech scenario applied on the constraint analysis of the aircraft was the most optimistic scenario after literature studies. The analysis was done and conclusions were drawn which led us to analyse the exploitation of solar power in isolated flight regimes and also took us to the possibility of a flying wing.
